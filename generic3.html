Facebook vision for the metaverse

Everything Facebook revealed about the Metaverse from saulat hussain qureshi on Vimeo.

Metaverse - Wake up the Shape from Joonmoe on Vimeo.

Later this year, the Metaverse, as we have come to know it, caught the attention of many. And one of the essential players scouting or at least known for the Metaverse is Facebook. It is even now called Meta. According to FB 2021, Mark Zuckerberg laid out the vision of the Metaverse as the successor to the mobile internet â€” a set of interconnected digital spaces that lets you do things you cannot do in the physical world. Other giant tech companies like Apple, Google and others also complement Facebook's vision for the Metaverse. Furthermore, the excitement for the future of the Metaverse has attracted investors, tech enthusiasts, professors and entrepreneurs. Google, just like other huge companies, believes that the Metaverse is just an extension of virtual reality or augmented reality. Nevertheless, Facebook thinks otherwise. Zuckerberg believes that the Metaverse is just a continuation of the company's mission and social apps today rather than being something entirely different. As he clearly stated, "In our DNA, we build technology to bring people together. The Metaverse is the next frontier in connecting people, just like social networking was when we got started." This vision, of course, might seem too far-fetched, but we know from history that FB has done a lot in bringing the world closer and making the world a better place for everyone. We have seen some of the products they have made and others still in the pipeline, like the VR Oculus and Facebook augmented reality glasses.

Facebook vision for the Metaverse is a lot more complex and broad. Moreover, it sought to connect all apps and anything digital. According to him, people will wear glasses that overlay information like directions, notable places (businesses, landmarks, etc.), and people's names (as may be limited by people you had previously met before and exchanged contact information with). The other significant promise is floating screens that could be how (when paired with a keyboard for typing) you work. There was a time when smartwatches were hyped to replace smartphones. In retrospect, that was never going to happen, as wrist-worn wearables could never be productivity devices for reading and heavy text input. So the question we should be asking is, will Facebook make releases of meta products before the other giants like Apple, Google and dominate as they have done. I think the Metaverse will be more like a collaboration than a competition. Because let us face it, each big company depends on one way or the other on other companies. I am just speculating -- this might happen or not. But we pray it does. Let us know what you think about it.
